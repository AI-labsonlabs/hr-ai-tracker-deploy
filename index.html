Methodology

This tracker evaluates HR AI adoption across 96 companies by collecting, classifying, and scoring public signals. Below is a complete breakdown of how scores are computed.

How Signal Scoring Works

Every signal gets a point value calculated from five independent factors multiplied together:

points = Signal Type × Confirmation Level × Source Credibility × Time Decay × Confidence

Signal Type and Confirmation Level are independent dimensions. Signal Type captures what kind of evidence was found (a deployment vs. a job post vs. a press release). Confirmation Level captures how verified that evidence is (confirmed vs. pilot vs. just exploring).

For example, the name “Confirmed Deployment” appears in both — but they mean different things. The signal type “Confirmed Deployment” (base weight 10.0) means the evidence itself is about a real deployment. The confirmation level “Confirmed Deployment” (×1.0) means that evidence has been verified as real.

Why this matters: A vendor case study (type weight 5.0) with Confirmed Deployment verification (×1.0) from an earnings call (source 1.0) scores much higher than a PR mention (type weight 1.0) at Exploration level (×0.4) from social media (source 0.3).

Example comparison:
Vendor case study, Confirmed: 5.0 × 1.0 × 0.85 = 4.25
PR mention, Exploration: 1.0 × 0.4 × 0.3 = 0.12
35× difference before time decay and confidence.

Confirmation Levels

Independent of signal type — this multiplier reflects how verified the evidence is:

Confirmed Deployment — ×1.0
A specific AI tool or platform is verified as running in production at meaningful scale (e.g., enterprise-wide rollout, confirmed in earnings call or vendor case study).
Pilot — ×0.7
A named tool is actively being tested in limited scope — one business unit, region, or use case — with intent to evaluate for broader rollout.
Preparing — ×0.5
The company is building toward AI adoption through infrastructure, hiring, or vendor procurement, but no specific tool is confirmed as deployed or piloted yet. Includes roles focused on building AI for HR.
Exploration — ×0.4
The company has expressed interest in HR AI through public statements, conference appearances, or generic leadership hires, but no concrete tool, vendor, or timeline has been identified.
PR Mention — ×0.15
AI is mentioned in press materials or blog posts without specifics about tools, timelines, or measurable outcomes. Often aspirational language without evidence of real activity.

Maturity Score (0–100)

Measures how deeply and broadly a company has adopted HR AI:

Step 1: Score each signal
points = base_weight × confirmation × source × decay × confidence
Step 2: Group by HR domain
7 domains: Recruiting, L&D, HR Ops, Comp/Benefits, Employee Service, Governance, AI Hiring. Each scored 0–100 independently with diminishing returns within a domain (1/√rank). Domains with at least one signal score a minimum of 1.
Step 3: Blend for overall score
base = (avg_all_7 × 0.6) + (avg_top_3 × 0.4)
60% rewards breadth across domains, 40% rewards depth in strongest areas.
Step 4: Company adjustments
final = base × √(size_norm) × √(regulatory_adj)
Smaller firms get a boost per signal (capped at 2×). Highly regulated industries get credit for operating in constrained environments.
Momentum Score (−10 to +10)

Measures whether adoption is accelerating or decelerating:

12-month avg. activity — Baseline: average weighted signal points per month over the past year.
Last 30 days (size-adj.) — Recent weighted signals, adjusted by company size normalization.
Change vs. baseline — How much the last 30 days exceeds or trails the 12-month average. Positive = accelerating.
Recent activity trend — Weighted across 30/60/90-day windows (30d ×3, 60d ×2, 90d ×1), emphasizing the most recent activity.
Final mapping
raw = (change × 0.6) + (trend × 0.4)
momentum = tanh(raw / 4) × 10
tanh smoothly maps raw values to −10 … +10, compressing extreme values. In practice, scores rarely exceed ±8 because tanh flattens as it approaches its limits.
Adoption Pattern Classification

Classified based on where signal weight concentrates:

Governance
Oversight signals + 30% of earnings mentions
Tool
Deployments + pilots + procurement
Talent
HR AI leadership hires + upskilling
AI Hiring
AI/ML engineer & data scientist hiring — separated from Talent to prevent distortion (~48% of all signals)
PR
PR-only mentions
If PR exceeds 50% of total weight → “PR-led”. Otherwise, the highest category wins. If two categories are within 10% of each other, both are listed (e.g., “Governance & Tool-led”).
Time Decay

Signals lose relevance over time using exponential decay with a 180-day half-life. A signal from 6 months ago contributes half the weight of an identical signal from today. This ensures the tracker reflects current adoption posture rather than historical activity.
